Wavy Beaches

Nilaveli Beach


Nilaveli Beach is a wide, sandy beach with palm trees and fishing boats sprinkled about. It's a 30-minute drive from the city of Trincomalee in the northeastern portion of Sri Lanka. Due to its own little reef, this tiny island features tranquil beaches with modest waves. Despite its size, Nilaveli's beach is popular with both residents and visitors. There are lifeguards on duty, as well as several small eateries along the beach, the most of which specialize on seafood.


Trincomalee, as a center of Tamil culture, is also a nice place to visit for sightseeing. The majority of Sri Lankans speak Tamil, which is also the name of the ethnic group to which they belong. If Nilaveli's seclusion isn't enough, a boat ride to Pigeon Island National Park, just a half-mile off the shore, is an option. This lovely island is well renowned for its snorkeling and scuba diving, and there is an excellent diving school and a tour company that offers guided tours of the island near the beach.


The Anilana Nilaveli hotel is located on Nilaveli Beach, only steps from from the Pigeon Island boat dock. This charming hotel provides luxurious rooms and services at a reasonable cost. The hotel features an on-site spa, two huge pools, and a restaurant, and guest rooms are vast and light with floor-to-ceiling windows and private balconies.





Arugam Bay


Arugam Bay is stunning, with sparkling white sand and deep blue sea that gradually turns green as you move closer to the beach. The beach is recognized as a worldwide surf destination because of its attractiveness, which includes some of Sri Lanka's top surf breakers. This reputation attracts a large number of people, but it also indicates that the area has an excellent tourism infrastructure and is easily accessible. The curving beach is reminiscent of a Hawaiian beach, with a dry, sandy part near the ocean, followed by rougher areas with plants and a tree line.


The Spice Trail hotel is a nice, tiny luxury resort with private villa rooms right on Arugam Bay.nEach has its own tiny outside garden space with lounge chairs for relaxation. Ancient temples and Kumana National Park, famed for the numerous types of birds that dwell in or migrate through the area, are both neighboring attractions.





Mirissa Beach


Mirissa is the major fishing port on the island's south shore, located on the island's southernmost point. It's easy to go to because of its beauty and proximity to Colombo (the capital city). The capital's airport is roughly a two-hour drive away. The little beach town and its wide beach are both lovely, with a dense grove of palm palms around them. The village is located on a little hill behind the beach.


Mirissa Beach is formed like an arrow, with the arrow tip protruding into the water. The surf is quiet and safe to swim because of the natural jetty. The sea is incredibly pure, and the beaches are dazzling white with smooth, fine sand. Where the bottom is sandy, it's bright green, and where there are rocks or reef, it's dark blue. Mirissa is also an excellent site to go whale watching, which is best done between February and March.


The Morning Star hotel provides well-appointed hotel rooms with private balconies and four-poster beds, within a five-minute walk from the beach. On-site amenities include a pool, a restaurant with room service, and mango trees.
