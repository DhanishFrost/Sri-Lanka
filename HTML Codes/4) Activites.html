Adventurous Hikings


Most travellers would have fantasized of hiking in Sri Lanka at some time. The country's name alone inspires visions of lush, verdant rainforests, rolling mountains, tropical fauna, and blue lakes. Sri Lanka's greatest hiking exemplifies this. Lonely Planet called the country the "greatest place in the world" for travelers to visit in 2019. They praised its "abundant and easily accessible animals," as well as the surf scene, culture, and people's kindness. Hiking usually cost around $20 to $100, and the best time to go is early in the morning or late in the afternoon, depending on the location. Below is the top 3 hiking places in Sri Lanka :

  1. Hike through the Knuckles Mountain Range.
  2. Hike to the Stunning Sinharaja Forest Reserve.
  3. Adam's Peak the best spiritual hike.

Amazing Safaries


Sri Lanka offers some of the most numerous nature preserves and nature reserves in the world, offering tourists to partake in some of the most exciting safari activities. Safaris in Sri Lanka are a great way to get a glimpse of the country's wild side


The wildlife of Sri Lanka, which includes elephants, leopards, a variety of deers, and wild boars, will astonish you at every turn on your journey. A safari in Sri Lanka costs around $10 to $20 per adult, and the best months to go are June to September, when the park is open from 6 a.m. to 6 p.m. and there are two safaris each day, one in the morning and one in the afternoon.

Here are three fantastic safari destinations in Sri Lanka.

  1. Yala National Park
  2. Udawalawe National Park
  3. Horton Plains National Park

Whale Watching


Blue whales are the largest mammals that have ever existed on Earth. And if you ever needed another incentive to visit Sri Lanka, this is it. Sri Lanka is properly claiming its own cetacean crown, despite not being as well-known as Antarctica or the Azores for large, gorgeous blues. And you won't be able to witness our ocean's most magnificent creatures one day and elephants or leopards the next in other regions of the whale-watching globe.


You don't have to embark on a grand voyage across vast oceans to have a wonderful time on the south coast of the Indian Ocean's renowned stunning 'Teardrop Island' and seeing blue whales off the coasts of Mirissa or Galle in Sri Lanka would almost surely bring you to tears. Two of the greatest spots to see blue whales are listed here. The prices will range from 50 to 200 dollars depending on the number of passengers and the most ideal time to visit is between December-April.

Here are two of the popular places to go on whale watching :

  1. Whale watching in Mirissa
  2. Whale watching in Kalpitiya
