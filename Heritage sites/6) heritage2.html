About Temple of Tooth

Kandy City is in the Central Highlands, near the center of Sri Lanka's small island. Sri Dalada Maligawa, popularly known as the Temple of the Tooth, is the town's crown treasure. The temple is part of the palace complex, which is known as 'Maligawa'. The entire edifice is at least two or three centuries old, and it has witnessed the rise and fall of numerous rulers. It has witnessed both brutal conflicts and calm reigns, but it is one of the island's most historically significant locations.

Architecture


The entire temple complex is constructed in the Kandyan style. The major shrine is divided into two levels. On each floor, there are several chambers, including one where the tooth relic known as 'handun kunama' is stored (inner sanctum). The tooth is kept in a special temple inside the two-story sanctuary, which is guarded by two huge elephant tusks. The relic is enclosed in jeweled caskets that sit on the throne and sits on a solid gold lotus blossom.


Despite the fact that portions of the shrine have been destroyed and rebuilt as a result of previous bombings and acts of war, the structure remains a wonderful example of luxury, grandeur, and splendor. The interior, as well as the outside, are decorated with golden architecture and moonstones, evoking the rich Buddhist past.



Main Attraction of the Temple


The sacred tooth relic is housed in a stupa that weighs 3,500 kilograms and is constructed of 320 kilograms of pure gold, the majority of which was given by worshippers. It's on the fourth story, in a room named 'Sacred Hall of Light.' Visitors are not permitted to enter the inner chamber, although they are permitted to observe the stupa from the public viewing area.



Entrance Fee & Facilities


In Sri Lanka, not all temples charge an admission fee. Temples that are famous tourist destinations, on the other hand, do charge an admission fee. Locals can come for free, but foreigners must pay a fee of $10. If you've done your homework ahead of time, you may take a self-guided tour of the entryway, lower hall, upper hall, palace, and museum.


You may engage a freelance guide for two hours if you want to learn more about the history and culture of the area. Many of these may be found at the temple's entrance. At the ticket counter, there are also free audio tours available. For a group of four, a guided tour of the entire temple complex costs $3.5.
